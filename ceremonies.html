Temple Ceremonies

Ceremonies deepen our relationship with the Three Treasures—the Buddha, the Law, and the Priesthood—and help us advance toward enlightenment together.

Oko Ceremony

A monthly ceremony that honors Nichiren Daishonin’s life and teachings. Members listen to a sermon from the chief priest and rededicate themselves to kosen-rufu.

Gojukai (Conferral of the Precepts)

The formal ceremony in which one becomes a Nichiren Shoshu believer, taking refuge in the Gohonzon and vowing to uphold the Law.

Tozan Pilgrimage

Visiting Head Temple Taisekiji in Japan to worship the Dai-Gohonzon. Pilgrimages are organized regularly—ask the temple office for dates.

Memorial Services

We chant for loved ones who have passed, transferring merit and renewing our vow to attain Buddhahood together.

Preparing for Ceremony

  • Arrive 15 minutes early to settle your mind and review etiquette.
  • Wear respectful, modest clothing. Business casual is encouraged.
  • Bring your Gongyo book and Juzu beads if you have them. Loaner sets are available.
  • Invite friends to observe introductory portions—contact the office to confirm which ceremonies include guest orientations.
